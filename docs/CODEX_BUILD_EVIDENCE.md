## Key creator decisions

The creator selected Dissent Garden from several concepts and directed its
product evolution. The defining decisions were human-owned:

- serve product and engineering teams making reversible decisions;
- preserve consequential dissent instead of manufacturing consensus;
- bind model claims to user-supplied evidence IDs;
- keep corrections and history append-only;
- use receipts to make memory tighter instead of longer;
- extend decisions through time with a plant-and-grow metaphor;
- enforce explicit token and dollar ceilings before model work begins.

Codex challenged, implemented, and verified those choices; it did not replace
the entrant's product judgment or ownership.
